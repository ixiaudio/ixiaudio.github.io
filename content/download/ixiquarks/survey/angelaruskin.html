User Feedback on ixiQuarks

Dear people at Anglia Ruskin. I was happy to hear from Julio d'Escrivan that you had been using ixiQuarks as part of your studies. I, Thor Magnusson, the developer of the software, am finishing my PhD thesis these days and would love to ask you a few questions regarding how you use the software (or not).

I would be delighted if you would take a few minutes to reply to this questionnaire. Even if you hate the guts of this software, let me know. (I won't come after you). User feedback will only make the software better and I will be releasing lots of new stuff in the spring, so your comments will help me to make better software.

You don't need to answer all the sections. However, we would like to have as much details as possible.

Discursive Questions

Roughly how often have you used ixiQuarks? Do you use it regularly? Where does it fit in your workflow? In what context? (live, studio) Do you use it with other software? If so, how?
Describe your overall reaction/experience when first encountering the software.
How do the ixiQuarks relate to your studies and work with SuperCollider? Do you feel it integrates well with SuperCollider?
Do you think ixiQuarks is a useful software for learning in a course like yours?
Can you describe how you use ixiQuarks in the compositional process?
Does the ixiQuarks environment affect your compositional or performative patterns? Do you think differently about your music? Does the environment encourage new directions in your music? If so, how?
Would you say that the software is general or specific? Discuss.
When using the ixiQuarks, do you often find that the software does not allow you to perform certain basic musical things? What is lacking? If not, are these limitations perhaps inspiring?
Do you find ixiQuarks easy or difficult to understand? To what degree do you feel that your way of thinking matches that of the designers of ixiQuarks?
What are the main features of ixiQuarks that you enjoy?
What, in your view is ixiQuarks best for?
