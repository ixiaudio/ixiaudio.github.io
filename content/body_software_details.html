the ixi software is free for you to download.

The ixi apps do not have to be installed, so just run them from wherever you want on your harddrive. Every application has a Read Me file and it is important to check it out.

 

We have tried to build the ixi software to be loaded and played without straining your machine. They are very easy to install (just copy them anywhere in your computer)


So why would i want to use an ixi app live?
As laptop computers have become cheaper we've seen more and more laptop performances. Though no different to a DJ playing out, the audience always seem to be faced with a musician sitting behind a black book with a glowing apple mac icon - wondering what the hell he/she is doing. With ixi software, it is easy to record soundfiles directly and instantly start to work with them in an intuitive interface. The directness of the software is very clear and one can manipulate the samples in realtime.

With the current ixi apps - the performance is lived out through the interface components. If you rigg up a projector to the laptop, the audience can see you move the interface components around the screen and understand how you are in control of the medium. We're not saying that this is fascinating viewing for a 5 hour set, but definitely more inviting that what we've seen before!


How do I record my performances?
We recommend that in a live performance situation that you run as few applications as possible. So to record your performance we recommend that you connnect a recording device (eg. DAT or mini disc) to the outbound mixing desk or PA system.

 


So how does an ixi application fit in with my studio set up?
The current ixi programmes are standalone applications, they do not require any hosts or specific audio setup to operate. As long as your computer can process wav, aif or quicktime you can use the ixi applications.

The ixi applications process audio files, which are triggered by user interactions with the interface of the software. The sound generated is sent to the sound card and outputted to what ever speaker set you have.

To record the audio generated by the software you can do one of the following:

RECORDING
Output to external recording device such as aminidisk or dat recorder from you sound card.

Sound card setup.
Many PC sound cards have a nicetool that allows the user to record "what you hear" or "Sound Mix". This will pick up the audio of the ixi applictions before they reach the soundcard. You can run more than one ixi application at the same time and record the mix live.

Audio out to audio in.
Get a cable to patch the audio out of your computer to the audio in on the same computer. This inbound audio can then be captured on an audio editor such as soundforge, wave lab or goldwave. (see links page)

Virtual Audio Cable
We havent used it - but Virtual Audio Cable from http://www.ntonyx.com would enable you to play out from an ixi application and record into an audio editor such as soundforge, wave lab or goldwave. (see links page).

On Mac OS X, we recommend the SoundFlower by Cycling74, the company which makes Max/MSP, and that is a free software.


USING THE RECORDINGS
Once you've recorded the output of an ixi application, depending on how you did the recording (see above), you can convert it to wav, aif or whatever and drop it into your favorite sequencer or sampler. It's worth checking our links page for software you can use to manipulate the recording from your ixi software.

Once you're happy with the track - the next thing to do is to get in contact with ixi label - we would really like to hear what the results are.

[for WinZip users]
How to uncompress a folder and its subfolders:
1. Start WinZip and open the Zip you want to uncompress
2. Select Extract from the Actions menu
3. Be sure the Use folder names checkbox is checked
4. Type the destination folder, for example C:\VIRUSES
5. Press the Extract button
